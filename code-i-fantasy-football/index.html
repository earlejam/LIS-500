Make Sunday fun again

Tired of doing nothing but homework on Sundays? Fantasy Football inspires millions around the world to put off all other responsibilities for a day and watch the points roll in. Plus, there's also a game every Thursday and Monday night.

Everybody's doing it

Families. Roommates. High School friends. Student Organizations. Coworkers. Fantasy Football is universally beloved by all who have played, and the lessons learned through team ownership can be readily applied to daily life.

What is it?

  • >>   Fantasy football is a competition where a group of people draft "fantasy" or imaginary teams consisting of NFL players that are capable of scoring points, and compete weekly against different teams to decide whose team is best.
    • dvr
  • >>   Each eligible NFL player can only be owned by a single team (person) in the league, which means owners must be strategic in which players they draft, and how early they're willing to draft such players.
    • help_outline
  • >>   A team's roster consists of "Starters" and "Bench Players", and each owner must make weekly decisions as to which players they believe will score the most points that week. A typical starting roster usually consists of a quarterback, two running backs, two wide receivers, a tight-end, a team defense, and a field-goal kicker.
    • thumbs_up_down
  • >>   To decide the winner of any season, the teams with the better head-to-head records during the first two-thirds of the season play one another in the last third of the season, the "playoffs", in a single-elimination format, until only one team remains.
    • trending_up

ESPN provides a convenient online draft tool so owners can draft from anywhere in the world.

Getting Started

Once you've bought into the idea of fantasy football, you'll be itching to create a league of your own. Here's how.
Step 1: Find your league members

Most leagues consist of between 8-12 owners, so get out there and find some friends, coworkers, family members or roommates to join in the fun.

Step 2: Create your League

After specifying some basic information like the name of your league and some basic scoring settings, your league will be up and running, albeit empty, at this point.

Jargon

NFL fans will be familiar with most of the fantasy lingo, but there are a few terms exclusive to the fantasy realm.
  • wrap_text Snake Draft - A draft format where the drafting order reverses at the end of each round.
  • chat_bubble Auction Draft - An advanced draft format where each owner has a budget, and players are nominated and auctioned off.
  • grade Stud - A player who performs consistenly well, and can be started every week.
  • cancel Bust - A player who underperforms each week, or has suffered a season-ending injury early in the season.
  • swap_vert Streaming - A strategy for roster management where the unclaimed "free agent" players are heavily relied upon.
  • exposure_plus_1 PPR - A popular scoring setting in which an additional point is awarded for each reception, in addition to yards and touchdowns.
  • lock Handcuff - A 2nd-string running back who backs up one of the top running backs, a "safety net" in case of injury.
